Research Interests

I'm interested in understanding and building foundations of generative models and applications of LLMs in particular.

Towards this goal, my research interests include: efficient sampling, fine-tuning, incontext learning, and evaluation, borrowing ideas from algorithms, causal inference, reinforcement learning, and statistics.

Lately I have been interested in solving it with a full stack approach:

1. Building agents for data science: in particular role of context management in multi-turn conversations by understanding the role of memory, efficient tool planning and also how to optimize for cost and latency.

2. Building tools for agents: building robust NL2SQL models through incontext learning and fine-tuning. Building tools that would be useful for time series analysis (anomaly detection, root cause analysis) and machine learning (classification, regression) as opposed to using NL2Code for these tasks.

3. Evaluation: how do we evaluate content generated using generative models (LLMs, diffusion models) by drawing connections to causal inference. I'm also interested in designing metrics and building models for video evaluation.

During my Ph.D., among other things, I have worked on identifying new connections between combinatorial optimization and causal inference.
